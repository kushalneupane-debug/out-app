Privacy Policy
Effective: May 25, 2026 · Applies globally, including GDPR (EU/EEA) and CCPA (California)

1. Who We Are

Out ("we", "us") is a real-time proximity-matching service. We are the data controller for personal data processed through this application. Contact: privacy@getout.app

2. What We Collect and Why

  • First name — you provide this; used only to identify you to nearby users during a session.
  • Activity vibe — your chosen activity (coffee, walk, etc.); used for matching and displayed to nearby users.
  • Approximate location — your GPS coordinates, immediately fuzzed by a random ±500 m offset before any processing. We never store or transmit your precise coordinates.

We do not collect email addresses, phone numbers, photos, government IDs, or any other identifying information.

3. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

We rely on legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) to operate the proximity-matching feature, and on your explicit consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) for processing location data, which you grant when you allow browser geolocation access. You may withdraw consent at any time by revoking location permissions in your browser settings and going offline.

4. Data Retention — Ephemeral by Design

All presence data (name, vibe, fuzzed location) is held only in active memory via Supabase Realtime. It is automatically and permanently deleted the moment you close the app, disconnect, or your session times out. There is no database of users. We retain no logs containing personal data beyond 24 hours of anonymised error telemetry.

5. Third-Party Infrastructure

We use Supabase (supabase.com) to power real-time presence channels. Supabase processes data on your behalf under a Data Processing Agreement compliant with GDPR. Supabase infrastructure is hosted on AWS (US-East-1 by default; EU regions available). No personal data is sold, rented, or shared with advertisers.

6. Your Rights

  • Access & portability — because we hold no persistent data, there is nothing to export. Sessions are anonymous and ephemeral.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten") — going offline or closing the browser immediately erases all your data. You may also contact us to confirm no residual data exists.
  • Objection / restriction — you may stop using the service at any time; no account closure is required.
  • Lodge a complaint — EU/EEA residents may complain to their local supervisory authority. California residents may contact the California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA).

7. Children's Privacy

Out is not directed at children under 13 (or 16 in the EU where applicable). We do not knowingly collect data from minors. If you believe a minor has used the service, contact privacy@getout.app and we will take immediate action.

8. Security

All data is transmitted over TLS 1.2+. Location data is fuzzed client-side before transmission. We apply the principle of data minimisation — we collect only what is strictly necessary for the service to function. No passwords or sensitive credentials are stored.

9. Changes to This Policy

We will post updates here. For material changes we will display a notice in the app. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.

Safety

Your safety is the product

Out is designed to help you meet people spontaneously. That's exciting — and it requires a few common-sense habits to keep it positive for everyone.

Before you meet

  • Tell someone. Let a friend or family member know where you're going and who you're meeting.
  • Public first. Always suggest a public place for a first meetup — a café, park, or busy street.
  • Keep it on-app. There's no need to share your phone number, social media, or address before meeting.
  • Trust your gut. If something feels off, it probably is. You don't owe anyone a meeting.

In the app

  • Block instantly. Any user can be blocked with one tap. They disappear from your map and you disappear from theirs — no notification sent.
  • Report abuse. Use the report button for harassment or dangerous behaviour. Three reports in 24 hours result in automatic removal from the network.
  • Your location is fuzzed. We add a ±500 m random offset before sharing any location data. Nobody — including us — knows your precise coordinates.
  • Auto-delete. Your presence data is permanently deleted the moment you go offline. Nothing persists.

If you feel unsafe

  • Emergency: Call your local emergency number (911 in the US, 999 in the UK, 112 in the EU).
  • Crisis support: Crisis Text Line — text HOME to 741741 (US).
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233.
  • Report to us: safety@getout.app — urgent reports are reviewed within 2 hours.

Community standards

Out is for spontaneous, genuine human connection. Harassment, unwanted advances, threats, or any form of discrimination based on race, gender, sexuality, religion, or disability will result in immediate and permanent removal. We take this seriously.

Cookie Policy
Effective: May 26, 2026 · Applies globally, including EU ePrivacy Directive and GDPR

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. They help websites remember information about your visit. We use a minimal set of cookies strictly required to operate the service.

2. Cookies We Use

  • Authentication cookie — set by Firebase when you sign in with Google. Stores your session token so you don't have to sign in again on every visit. Expires after 1 hour of inactivity or when you close the app. Strictly necessary.
  • Supabase session — a short-lived token stored in localStorage (not a cookie) used to maintain your real-time presence channel while you are live. Deleted automatically when you go offline or close the browser tab. Strictly necessary.

We do not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, analytics cookies, or any third-party marketing cookies.

3. No Cookie Consent Banner Required

Under the EU ePrivacy Directive and GDPR, cookies that are strictly necessary for a service you have requested do not require prior consent. Because we use only strictly necessary cookies, no cookie banner is shown. If we ever introduce optional cookies we will update this policy and ask for your consent before setting them.

4. Third-Party Services

  • Firebase (Google) — handles authentication. Google may set its own cookies for sign-in. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Supabase — handles real-time matching. Uses localStorage, not cookies. See Supabase's Privacy Policy.

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Deleting the authentication cookie will sign you out. Common browser guides:

  • Chrome
  • Firefox
  • Safari

6. Changes to This Policy

If we introduce new cookies or change how we use existing ones, we will update this page and, where required by law, ask for your consent. The effective date at the top of this page always reflects the most recent version.
